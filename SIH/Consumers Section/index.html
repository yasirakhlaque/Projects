Consumer Protection Laws

A guide to your rights as a consumer

Return and Refund Policies

If you buy something that turns out to be faulty, doesn’t do what it’s supposed to, or isn’t of acceptable quality, you have the right to ask for a repair, replacement, or refund. This applies to products and services.

Misleading Advertisements

Businesses are not allowed to make false or misleading claims about their products or services. This includes advertisements, product packaging, and any statements made by a business’ staff.

Product Safety Standards

All products sold in Australia must meet strict safety standards. If a product is found to be unsafe, businesses must recall the product and offer consumers a refund or replacement.

What to do if you have a problem

Contact the business to explain the problem and what you want them to do about it. If you’re not able to resolve it with the business, you can report it to your local consumer protection agency.
